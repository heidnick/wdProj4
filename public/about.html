Description of tools:

APIs Used:

  • Leaflet: Javascript library used to build the interactive map with zooming, panning, markers, etc.
  • Nominatim: Tool used to convert addresses to latitude and longitude, and vice versa.
  • St. Paul Crime API: RESTful server used to query information from the St. Paul Crime database.

Frameworks Used:

  • Bootstrap: Popular, front end framework used for layout and styling for headers, tables, buttons, etc.

Interesting things discovered:

  • 1: Out of all the neighborhoods, it seems like Payne/Phalen is the most dangerous to live in, having the most occurences of assault, theft, rape, burglary, and robbery.
  • 2: Thomas/Dale seems to have the most drug users, as the number of incidents with narcotics is 204, significantly higher than all other neighborhoods.
  • 3: Capitol River seems to be the most graffitied section of St. Paul. From 2014 - 2020, there were 101 incidents of graffiti.
  • 4: From 2014 - 2020, there has been a big increase in the number of proactive police visits. It's good that police are more often trying to stop crime before it happens.
  • 5: The number of homicides is unfortunately on the rise in St. Paul. 2014-2016 had only a couple homicides, whereas 2017, 2018, and 2019 had 6, 4, and 10 respectively.
  • 6: The number of rapes in St. Paul is higher than the national average. For 2015 - 2020, there have been around 150 rapes or more per year.
